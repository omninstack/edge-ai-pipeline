# 5.1 Objectives

## Primary Goals
* **Safe & Secure OTA:** Reliable remote updates for firmware and AI models.
* **Fleet Orchestration:** Managing distributed edge infrastructure at scale.
* **Industrial Validation:** Proving reliability under harsh physical and electrical conditions.
* **Deterministic Deployment:** Ensuring zero-downtime or safe-state updates.
* **Continuous Monitoring:** Real-time observability of device health and AI performance.

# 5.3 Over-The-Air (OTA) Strategy

## Deployment Targets
* **Firmware OTA:** Full system image or application-level updates.
* **AI Model OTA:** Hot-swapping quantized TFLite/ONNX models without full reboots.
* **Configuration OTA:** Updating MQTT parameters, sensor thresholds, or device logic.
* **Security OTA:** Certificate rotation and security patch distribution.

## Safety & Reliability Mechanisms
* **Dual-Partition (A/B) Boot:** Always maintaining a known-good backup image.
* **Atomic Activation:** New images are only activated after bit-for-bit integrity validation (SHA-256).
* **Automatic Rollback:** Reverting to the previous version if the new app fails to heartbeat within a timeout.
* **Resumable Downloads:** Support for intermittent or low-bandwidth field connectivity (LTE/LoRa).

# 5.4 Edge Orchestration & Fleet Management

Managing the lifecycle of thousands of distributed devices.

## Fleet Operations
* **Zero-Touch Provisioning:** Automated registration and secure identity assignment.
* **Group Management:** Deploying updates to specific sites, device types, or staging groups.
* **Remote Diagnostics:** Pulling logs and triggering remote diagnostic routines.

## Orchestration Stack
* **Micro-Containers:** Docker/Docker Compose for gateway-level service management.
* **Lightweight K8s:** k3s for advanced multi-service orchestration on high-performance gateways.
* **Messaging Backbone:** MQTT for low-latency command and control.

# 5.5 Industrial Field Validation

Proving the system survives and performs in the "Real World."

## Validation Categories
| Category | Focus | Test Scenarios |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Environmental** | Physical Stress | Temperature (-20 to 70°C), EMI/EMC, Vibration. |
| **Network** | Connectivity | LTE Signal drops, High packet loss, MQTT Broker outages. |
| **Stress** | System Load | Max protocol polling rates + concurrent AI inference. |
| **Safety** | Fail-Safe Logic | Power loss during OTA, sensor disconnection, AI confidence drops. |

## AI Reliability Testing
Validating model performance against actual industrial noise sources:
* **False Alarm Rejection:** Testing fire models against welding smoke, dust, and steam.
* **Drift Monitoring:** Detecting when model accuracy degrades due to changing machine behavior over months.

# 5.7 Continuous Observability (MLOps)

* **Telemetry:** Real-time stream of CPU, Memory, Temperature, and Signal Strength.
* **AI Insights:** Tracking inference results, confidence scores, and processing times.
* **Log Aggregation:** Centralized collection of edge logs via Loki or ELK.
* **Alerting:** Automated notifications for device offline, AI drift, or hardware fault.

---

# 5.8 Recommended Validation Toolchain

* **Automated Testing:** PyTest (Logic), Robot Framework (E2E).
* **Load Testing:** Locust (MQTT/API Stress).
* **OTA Frameworks:** Mender, Balena, ESP-IDF OTA, RAUC.
* **Observability:** Grafana (Dashboards), Prometheus (Metrics), Loki (Logs).
