## Edge AI Use Case Definition — Industrial Automation & Data Acquisition Platform

### 1. Overview

The Edge AI platform provides a scalable, low-latency, and secure industrial automation and data acquisition infrastructure. The system integrates field devices, industrial protocols, local AI inference, and cloud connectivity to support real-time monitoring, analytics, predictive maintenance, and autonomous control.

The architecture is structured into functional layers:

* **Edge Node Layer** → Protocol adaptation and sensor interfacing
* **Gateway Layer** → Orchestration, data aggregation, and advanced analytics
* **Industrial Pipeline** → MQTT-centric data flow
* **Digital Twin & AI** → Data-driven diagnostics and simulation

# 3. Key Edge AI Use Cases

## A. Predictive Maintenance
* **Inputs:** Vibration, temperature, current, motor RPM, pressure.
* **Edge AI Functions:** Anomaly detection, trend prediction, Remaining Useful Life (RUL) estimation.
* **Benefits:** Prevention of unexpected shutdowns, maintenance cost reduction, scheduled servicing optimization.

## B. Real-Time Alarm Classification
* **Inputs:** Smoke detectors, thermal sensors, gas sensors, vision sensors.
* **Edge AI Functions:** False alarm reduction, multi-sensor fusion, local emergency decision-making.
* **Benefits:** Faster response times, reduced false positives, reliable offline operation.

## C. Industrial Protocol Gateway Intelligence
* **Inputs:** Modbus RTU/TCP, BACnet MS/TP/IP, EtherNet/IP, CAN bus.
* **Edge AI Functions:** Protocol normalization, intelligent data filtering, semantic tagging, data quality scoring.
* **Benefits:** Unified data model, reduced engineering complexity, seamless Digital Twin integration.

## D. Energy Optimization
* **Inputs:** Power meters, HVAC load, equipment runtime, environmental sensors.
* **Edge AI Functions:** Peak load prediction, smart load shedding, energy anomaly detection.
* **Benefits:** Lower energy consumption, reduced peak demand charges, automated sustainability reporting.

## Processing Strategy

### Local Edge Processing (Critical)
Executed directly on Edge Nodes and Gateways to ensure safety logic, AI inference, device control, and immediate alarms.

### Cloud Processing (Non-Critical)
Reserved for historical analytics, fleet management, long-term AI training, Digital Twin visualization, and enterprise-level dashboards.

---

# 5. Privacy & Security Constraints

## Data Privacy
* **Local Data Ownership:** Sensitive industrial data (production metrics, machine parameters, alarm logs) remains on-premise.
* **Selective Upload:** Only aggregated telemetry, high-level events, and system health summaries are sent to the cloud.

## Security Architecture
* **Device Security:** Secure boot, signed firmware, hardware root of trust (TPM/Secure Element).
* **Communication Security:** TLS 1.2/1.3, MQTT over TLS, certificate-based authentication.
* **Access Control:** Role-Based Access Control (RBAC), device identity management, API authentication, and audit logging.
* **AI Security:** Model signing, secure OTA updates, and runtime integrity checks.

# 8. AI Deployment Strategy

## TinyML on Edge Nodes
Suitable for simple anomaly detection, signal classification, and sensor fusion.
* **Target:** Microcontrollers (e.g., ESP32, STM32).
* **Frameworks:** TensorFlow Lite Micro, Edge Impulse, ESP-DL.

## AI Runtime on Gateways
Suitable for multi-device analytics, time-series prediction, and complex inference pipelines.
* **Target:** Linux-based Gateways / High-performance MCUs.
* **Platforms:** ONNX Runtime, TensorRT, OpenVINO.

---

# 9. Reliability & Scalability

## System Resilience
* **Offline Operation:** Autonomous operation during network outages.
* **Data Integrity:** Local buffering and store-and-forward mechanisms.
* **High Availability:** Watchdog recovery, dual firmware partitions, and heartbeat monitoring.

## Scalability Targets
* **Capacity:** 100–500 devices per gateway.
* **Throughput:** 10k+ MQTT messages/sec.
* **Extensibility:** Pluggable protocol adapters and OTA-updatable AI models.
